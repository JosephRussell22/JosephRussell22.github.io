Phylogenies: Understanding Species Relationships

Phylogenies are diagrams or trees that depict the evolutionary relationships between different species. They are constructed based on various types of data, including morphology (physical characteristics), genetics, and behavior.

Physicallogenies: Representing Morphological Relationships

Physicallogenies specifically focus on representing the relationships between species based on their morphology, or physical characteristics. These characteristics can include features such as body shape, size, skeletal structure, and other observable traits.

In a physicallogeny, species that share more similar physical characteristics are placed closer together on the tree, indicating a closer evolutionary relationship. Conversely, species with more distinct physical traits are placed farther apart.
